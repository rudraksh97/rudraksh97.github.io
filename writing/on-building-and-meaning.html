On building, and why it feels like meaning

2026-06-28

When I'm deep in building something — a service, a feature, a small tool that does one thing well — the background hum of anxiety goes quiet. I've come to think that isn't a coincidence. Making things might be the most honest way I know to spend attention.

Building is thinking made concrete

An idea in your head can stay vague forever. The moment you try to build it, reality pushes back: the edge cases surface, the hand-waving collapses, the assumptions get tested. Building is just thinking that refuses to let you lie to yourself.

Meaning is a byproduct, not a target

I don't think you find meaning by searching for it directly. It shows up sideways — in the focus of solving a hard problem, in handing someone a thing that makes their day easier. Aim at the work; meaning arrives as a side effect.
